What is the YouTube Algorithm?

YouTube algorithm is an AI recommendation system that suggests videos to users based on user interactions and content relevancy. The major goal of the algorithm is to connect the users with the videos they find engaging and relevant. YouTube suggests videos that each viewer is most likely to watch and enjoy, using factors such as click-through rate, watch time, viewing habits, etc. According to research, YouTube’s recommendation algorithm drives 70% of what people watch on the platform. YouTube’s first algorithm aimed to recommend videos that attracted the most clicks. It has gradually evolved into a more intense algorithm as more users have signed up for the platform.

YouTube says the algorithm is a ‘Feedback loop in real-time that recommends videos to individual viewers according to their different interests’.

How YouTube Algorithm Works in 2026?

The YouTube recommendation system in 2025 is more inclined toward personalized video suggestions based on a viewer’s viewing habits, engagement, and other preferences. Two significant steps are involved for the YouTube algorithm to recommend videos to the viewers:

  1. Based on Performance Analytics
  2. Based on user’s past viewing habits

These YouTube recommendation algorithms are seen on YouTube in three major areas. Let’s look at them in detail:

Home Page

The home page is where the users first land after opening YouTube. The recommendations on the home page are based on the videos’ performance plus the user’s previous watch history. YouTube says ‘It’s where we deliver the most relevant, personalized recommendations to each viewer.’

Suggested Videos

The YouTube-suggested video algorithm is recommended alongside the video you are watching. In this area, YouTube specifically uses your current video combined with your watch history to deliver suggestions. Additionally, it considers the topic of your interest and liked channels and provides you with a customized list of video recommendations. Observing the suggested column next to your competitors’ content is advisable. If YouTube recommends videos there, try creating similar content to get recommended.
